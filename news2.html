Surf, Sand, and Sewage: What lies beneath the surface at Cornwall's most idyllic beaches

Header photo taken by Tamsin Hemming.

Written by Arian

In an extensive investigation into Cornwall's environmental challenges, our team meticulously studied the region's most polluted beaches, with a particular focus on three highly popular and widely frequented destinations. Seaton Beach, Trevone Beach, and Porthcurno Beach have long been cherished by beachgoers and hold immense significance in terms of recreational activities and environmental awareness.

Trevone Bay, positioned in the northern coast of Cornwall, is a surfing hotspot for both intermediate and experienced enthusiasts. Notably, it endured a significant 372 hours of sewage dumping throughout 2021, amounting to approximately two weeks with one event accounting for almost 100 hours . Despite this, the Environment Agency has bestowed upon it excellent water quality ratings for 2019, 2021, and 2022.

In south eastern Cornwall near Looe, Seaton Beach is a notable family-oriented site. It, too, faced substantial sewage dumping in 2021, with a total of 1054 hours, including a singular 264 hour incident. Yet, it upholds an excellent rating from the Environment Agency for water quality.

Porthcurno Beach, a part of Cornwall's far west, had 383 hours of sewage dumping in 2021, with one event accounting for almost 240 hours. Still, this location maintains its top water quality ratings from the Environment Agency.

These three beaches hold the doubtful distinction of being the worst in Cornwall in terms of sewage dumping hours for 2021. Nevertheless, according to the Environment Agency they still provide excellent water quality for their visitors. It's worth noting that the major sewage dumping incidents largely occur outside the peak bathing season, minimising the potential impact on the regular visitors of these picturesque settings.
